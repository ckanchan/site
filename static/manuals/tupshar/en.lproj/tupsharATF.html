Tupshar ATF

The Tupshar ATF (T-ATF) format allows users to enter special transliteration characters using only keys easily accesible on a standard QWERTY keyboard.

It is an adaptation of the ATF (ASCII Transliteration Format) in use at the Cuneiform Digital Library Initiative and Oracc projects. The one change T-ATF makes is to move trailing commas in digraphs to the leading position (e.g. to represent ṣ in T-ATF, write ,s instead of s,). This disambiguates between grammatical uses of the comma, such as when used to signify a clause break after the letter s, and a T-ATF digraph (the combinations ,s or ,t should not occur in normal usage). This enables T-ATF to be written inline in modern language prose more easily.
