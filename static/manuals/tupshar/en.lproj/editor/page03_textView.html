Using the text pane to view, select and delete

The Text pane provides four different views into your document:

  • Cuneiform
  • Transliteration
  • Normalisation
  • Word-by-word translation

You can change the view using the tab bar at the top of the text pane.

Selecting, editing and deleting lemmas

In the transliteration and normalisation views, you can select individual lemmas for editing using the mouse or keyboard. The mode line at the bottom of the pane reflects the selection status:

In this example, the second lemma of the second line has been selected, and its details are displayed in the Edit pane for modification. The mode line shows that when you press the + button or the Return key, the selected lemma will be overwritten with your changes. Alternatively, you can press the Backspace key to delete the selected lemma.

Inserting a lemma at a specific point

You can also insert a lemma at a specific point whilst in the transliteration or normalisation views. To do this, place the document insertion caret in between two lemmas:

If you place the insertion caret in the middle of a lemma, Tupshar assumes you want to insert before the lemma, not after. Otherwise, if you make a partial selection within the lemma, Tupshar counts it as a full selection and will assume that you want to make an edit.

When you place the insertion point, the mode line changes to show that when you press the + button or the Return key, it will insert a lemma at the caret point. In this example, the lemma will become the fourth lemma on the first line, and the following lemma positions will be incremented by one to allow for this.

Tupshar then sets the caret to its default position at the end of the document, ready for appending further lemmas. You can click in the text pane to re-place the caret for an insertion, editing or delete operation at this point.
